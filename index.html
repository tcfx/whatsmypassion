What's My Passion?

My passion is to help people bring underrepresented experiences to modern day art and advertising. I’ve noticed a lack of exploration of complex backgrounds and intersections in media production that reflects our current society. In my work I make an effort to research current events, themes, and experiences that go outside the norms. Two projects that highlight my passion for inclusion are Access Point and Atiba Descendants. Access Point is an expereince helps people with low vision or vision impairment navigate transit through sound. Access Point was created as a solution to the lack of accessiblity that transit, but its turning to solution can help anyone navigate transit and address common design issues that’s involved on transit. Atiba Descendants is photography project that showcases family, culture, and celebration. I wanted to show the beauty of African culture and the importance of preserving it. I also wanted to challenge the stereotypes that exist about black people espically Africans.
